Yash Dahima is a PhD candidate working on understanding the dynamics of urban and rural air quality (both ambient and indoor) - a crucial issue from policy perspectives. His research methodology incorporates synergetic use of low-cost sensors, research-grade instruments, and satellite remote sensing. He is also interested in exploring the applications of data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence in understanding air quality and climate change.
